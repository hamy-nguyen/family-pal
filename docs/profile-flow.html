Profile management

A profile is owned by exactly one principal — an account or a household — and granted to any number of others. These diagrams cover every finalized case.

Cases

Case → mechanism

Case Mechanism
Use the app individually Account-owned self-profile, no household
Track a family Household-owned member profiles
Your own health in the family book Account-owned + grant to the household
Someone joins your family (reconciliation) They grant their profile to your household
A person in two families (multi-family) Grant to multiple households — same record, both see it
Share one person with a doctor / teacher Grant to an account (viewer, optional expiry)
Child grows up (graduation) Transfer ownership: household → their account; keep or drop the family grant
Give a household-managed person their own login Transfer ownership between account and household
A member leaves or is removed Revoke their owned-profile grants — non-destructive, nothing deleted
Erase a person's records Delete profile (owner / editor) — hard delete, cascades visits
Rules

Invariants baked in

OwnershipExactly one owner per profile; many grants.
ControlOnly the owner manages a profile's grants and ownership — the anti-exfiltration guard.
Removal ≠ deletionLeaving/removing a member only revokes grants; records leave with their owner.
No orphansEvery household keeps ≥ 1 owner — guards block the last one from leaving.
